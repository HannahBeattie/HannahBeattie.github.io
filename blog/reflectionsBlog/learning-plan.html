P L A N

Here are the answers to the questions I was recently asked around my learning plan for dev academy (bootcamp).


My career goal is irrelevant right now. Instead, the goal I have is fall in love with programming; To learn some ways to sit with frustration. To gain enough understanding that I can notice and enjoy making-and-disrupting patterns-of-information. To find multiple ways to ‘solve’ a problem, and begin to get comfortable with ‘playing’ with code.
I hope to get to a stage where I can use these skills to amplify voices that need to be amplified, or make information more accessible to marginalised groups…
but for now, I am focusing on noticing how I feel and what I enjoy. I imagine my interests and curiosity will dive me from there.

My challenges in bootcamp may centre around my struggles to take in written-and-numeral content, self-doubt and ‘freezing up’ under pressure, or allowing other people to ‘rule over’ me in situations where they might have some socialised power.
I have a bunch of internalised misogyny and I am neuro-divergent in ways that can make learning institutes challenging, so I think communicating clearly, asking for help and not comparing myself to others will be very important.
I am pretty confident in my ability to respect boundaries, listen actively and supportively to others and work really hard…
I have an unusual approach to many things which can mean I struggle to grasp concepts that seem simple to others, but it also means I can come up with creative solutions (hurrah!).
I sometimes find it difficult working with other people, because I tend to ‘mask’ and that is exhausting… I can also sometimes try too hard to centre other people’s feelings, to the point where I overlook my own, so I am going to try to keep making space for myself and treat my own feelings with the respect that I treat other’s.
I feel pretty confident with my human skills, although practicing boundary-setting is always good. I generally feel good about my ability to treat everyone with empathy, patience and respect and allow people space/a voice…
sometimes it is hard to expect the same treatment from others however when my instinct is to ‘smooth things over’ or submit to dominant behaviour or aggressive communication styles. I hope I don’t experience this at Dev, but it would be a challenge…otherwise I am happy with my human skills.
I am on the ASD spectrum so I communicate ‘abnormally’, but I know who I am and how I like to treat people; And I can’t imagine altering those high expectations of myself or requiring ‘more’ of myself in that way.
I had to develop ‘human skills’ manually and deliberately so I have focused on them more than many neurotypical people I believe.

My expectation of the bootcamp team is to be supported in having a safe environment (ie. free of bullying/harassment/boundary pushing) and some flexibility and patience around the way I learn.

My expectation of myself prioritise my own safety and mental health over any other type of ‘success’.
I have a very high ethical standard of my own behaviour and treatment of people, that does not depend on how I feel about someone or how they treat me, so I don’t feel like I need to set those standards (my focus on therapy has been around controlling my overly-strong empathetic instinct so that I don’t tolerate abusive of boundary-pushing behaviours)…
I hope (but am not sure that I expect) to not take things too seriously, but see this bootcamp as a chance to play and enjoy noticing patterns and what they can do.
I am a hard worker and work best when I am learning through play and deliberate, enthusiastic observation…
So I think I need to divorce myself from seeing this as high-stakes; Because I know that I will respect the resources and knowledge that is being shared with me.
And I know I will respect and honour other students learning, and take my responsibilities in team work seriously… So it is important that I relax a little, and realise that this experience can be enjoyable, and I don’t need to punish or berate myself into learning.
